What a lecture partnership looks like

Here's a concrete example of how a guest lecture partnership might be structured within the nurse anesthesia program's didactic curriculum.

Regional anesthesia: ultrasound-guided nerve blocks

Example lecture partnership — illustrative only

ANE722 · Advanced Principles II
Sessions 2 × 90 minutes
Format In-person
Cohort size 10 students
Scheduling Coordinated around your OR schedule
Session 1

Foundations and patient selection

Anatomy review, ultrasound physics basics, indications and contraindications, patient selection framework, informed consent considerations, and adjuvant selection.

Session 2

Technique, complications, and failure management

Needle visualization, hydrodissection technique, recognition and management of local anesthetic systemic toxicity (LAST), block failure pathways, and real case discussion.

What we provide: Learning objectives aligned to COA standards, student pre-reading materials, slide template, post-session evaluation summary, and documentation of your contribution for your teaching portfolio and CV.

What makes a competitive APD candidate

The Assistant Program Director role requires COA-defined qualifications. Getting ahead of these now — even informally — will position you well when the posting opens.

Licensure

Active CRNA certification

Current NBCRNA certification and unrestricted Illinois APRN licensure required.

Education

Doctoral degree

A DNP in nurse anesthesia is required for the APD role.

Experience

Clinical depth

Demonstrated clinical expertise, ideally with breadth across specialty areas within the Carle system.

Scholarship

Teaching or scholarly activity

Evidence of teaching, precepting, committee work, publication, or professional presentation strengthens candidacy.
